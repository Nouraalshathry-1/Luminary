**Privacy Policy for Luminary**

*Last updated: [June 12, 2026]*

Luminary ("the App") is designed with your privacy in mind. This policy explains what information the App collects, how it is used, and how it is stored.

**Information We Collect**

Luminary collects the following information, all of which is entered or generated by you while using the App:

- Mood ratings before and after each walk
- Journal entries and reflections (free writing and guided question responses)
- Notes written during your walk
- Step count and walk duration, measured using your device's motion sensors
- Session timestamps and titles

**How Your Data Is Stored**

All data you create in Luminary is stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. If you have iCloud enabled, your data is also synced to your personal iCloud account via Apple's CloudKit, allowing it to appear across your own devices.

We do not have access to your data. Your mood entries, notes, and reflections are stored only on your device and in your personal iCloud account, which is controlled by you and governed by Apple's own privacy policy.

**Motion & Fitness Data**

Luminary uses your device's motion sensors (CoreMotion) to count steps during a walking session. This data is used only to display your step count within the App and is stored as part of your session history as described above.

**Data Sharing**

We do not sell, share, or transmit your personal data to any third party. Luminary does not include advertising, analytics, or tracking technologies.

**Data Deletion**

You can delete individual walk sessions and their associated notes at any time within the App. Deleting the App from your device will remove locally stored data; if iCloud sync is enabled, data may remain in your iCloud account until removed from all synced devices.

**Children's Privacy**

Luminary does not knowingly collect information from children under the age of 13.

**Changes to This Policy**

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected with an updated "Last updated" date above.
